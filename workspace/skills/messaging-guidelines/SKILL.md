Read this file **before composing any message** in lead-outreach, connection-outreach, linkedin-follow-up, email-follow-up, or lead-conversation. Every message sent on behalf of Mostly must follow these guidelines.

---

## Core objective

The goal is **not** to sound like:

- sales
- lead generation
- consulting
- AI outreach
- appointment setting

The goal is to **start natural conversations**, uncover operational pain points, make the lead feel understood, build curiosity gradually, and naturally transition toward a call or audit later.

The lead should feel: *"these guys work with businesses like ours all the time."*

---

## Tone and style

The message should feel:

- relaxed
- natural
- conversational
- slightly imperfect
- observational
- human

It should feel like **a founder or operator messaging another founder or operator** — not a salesperson, not a marketer, not an AI.

---

## Language — what to avoid

Do **not** use:

- em dashes
- polished copywriting
- perfect sentence structure
- corporate wording
- marketing buzzwords
- long paragraphs
- emojis
- "hope you're well"
- "book a call"
- "we help businesses scale"
- "streamline operations"
- "revolutionise"
- "increase efficiency"
- mentions of **AI**

Real people keep things short, do not over-explain, do not sound scripted, and do not try to sound smart.

---

## First message rules

The first message should **never**:

- explain Mostly
- mention AI
- pitch services
- ask for a call
- use a subject line like "Quick question" or "Introduction"

It should:

- reference a **real operational problem** common in their industry / vertical
- ask **one simple question**
- feel **short, casual, slightly imperfect, observational**
- open with **`Hey {name},`** (casual) or **`Hi {name},`** when slightly warmer fits

Do **not** over-personalise. One light signal from their headline, role, or company type is enough. No long riffs on their posts or profile.

## Industry verticals — openers and pain points

Use these as **patterns**, not copy-paste. Vary the wording each time. The question should match the lead's inferred vertical.

### Real estate

**Common pain points:** buyer follow-up, missed leads, database hygiene, enquiry response times, listing coordination, admin overload

**Example first-message questions (adapt):**

- "Hey James, curious how you're currently handling buyer follow-up internally?"
- "Hey Sarah, are you still managing most enquiry follow-up manually?"
- "Hey Michael, how are you currently managing lead response times?"
- "Hey Chris, are you still pretty hands on with database follow-up?"

---

### Trades / construction

**Common pain points:** quote follow-up, scheduling, admin overload, lead tracking, client communication, project coordination

**Example first-message questions (adapt):**

- "Hey David, how are you currently managing quote follow-up?"
- "Hey Chris, are you still handling most scheduling internally?"
- "Hey Michael, curious how you're managing leads/admin currently?"
- "Hey Sarah, how are you currently keeping on top of client communication across jobs?"

---

### Recruitment

**Common pain points:** candidate follow-up, lead tracking, database management, outbound consistency, admin overload

**Example first-message questions (adapt):**

- "Hey James, how are you currently managing candidate follow-up internally?"
- "Hey Sarah, are you still handling most outbound manually?"
- "Hey Michael, curious how you're managing database follow-up at the moment?"

---

### Clinics / health

**Common pain points:** appointment follow-up, missed enquiries, front desk overload, lead nurturing, retention

**Example first-message questions (adapt):**

- "Hey Chris, how are you currently managing enquiry follow-up internally?"
- "Hey Sarah, are you still handling most appointment follow-up manually?"
- "Hey Michael, curious how you're managing patient follow-up currently?"

---

### Agencies

**Common pain points:** client communication, onboarding, follow-up, backend systems, day-to-day ops

---

**If vertical is unclear:** default to one short question about follow-up, coordination, or admin load based on their headline. Still operational, still conversational.

## Message body rules (all messages)

- **2 to 4 short sentences** or even a single question with one line of context
- No filler ("Hope this finds you well", "I came across your profile")
- No bullet markers, no decorative separators (`-`, `_`, `---`) inside the message itself
- No escape sequences (`\n`, `\t`) in the actual sent text — use real line breaks in the composer
- Write like a human DM, not a template or cadence
- Do **not** paste line by line into the Sales Navigator composer — draft the full message first, then paste in one go

---

## Conversation framework

The conversation should **continue naturally**, uncover operational pain, build curiosity gradually, and avoid sounding salesy at every stage.

Move toward a call, quick audit, or discussing options **only after**:

- pain points are identified
- the lead is engaged
- the lead is asking questions or clearly acknowledging issues

### Conversation structure

1. Acknowledge what they said
2. Relate to similar businesses ("we hear that a lot", "pretty common with growing teams")
3. Validate the pain without labelling it as a problem to sell to
4. Ask a natural follow-up question to keep uncovering
5. Continue until pain is clear and engagement is real
6. Transition casually toward a call or audit

Every reply should **naturally open the next part of the conversation**.

## Early-stage reply examples

**Lead: "Yeah we still handle most of it manually."**

- "Yeah honestly we hear that a fair bit from similar businesses. Usually starts getting painful once things get busy. What part becomes the biggest headache for you guys?"
- "Seems pretty common. We've helped a few businesses clean that side up recently because things started getting messy as they grew. Is it more the follow-up side or the admin side causing issues?"
- "Yeah a lot of businesses are still doing it that way. Usually works fine until the volume picks up. Are things starting to get hard to stay on top of internally?"

**Lead: "Follow-up has been getting messy lately."**

- "Yeah that's usually the first thing that starts slipping once things get busy. Is it mainly speed to reply or keeping track of conversations?"
- "Seems pretty common once the business starts growing quickly. We've helped a few businesses tighten that side up recently. Are leads mainly coming through one channel or multiple?"
- "Yeah once the volume picks up it usually gets hard to stay on top of everything manually. Are enquiries slipping through or is it more just time consuming?"

**Lead: "Admin has become a nightmare."**

- "Honestly we hear that a lot. Usually means the business is growing quicker than the systems behind it. What side is taking up the most time currently?"
- "Yeah that seems pretty common with growing teams lately. Is it mainly scheduling, follow-up or just everything piling up?"
- "We've helped a few businesses simplify that side recently because things started becoming chaotic internally. What's causing the biggest headaches day to day?"

**Lead: "We've tried systems before."**

- "Yeah most businesses we speak with have tried bits and pieces at some point. Usually things just end up disconnected. What's been the biggest frustration for you guys?"
- "Hearing that a lot lately actually. Usually businesses end up with multiple tools that don't really work together properly. Was that the issue for you guys as well?"

---

## Later-stage transition (only when engagement and pain are clear)

Casual bridging lines before suggesting a call:

- "Honestly sounds pretty similar to a few businesses we've worked with recently."
- "We've seen a lot of similar setups lately with growing teams."
- "Usually there's a few pretty easy things that can be cleaned up once we look under the hood a bit."
- "Sounds like things have probably grown quicker than the systems behind it."

---

## Soft call / audit transition (only after multiple exchanges and clear engagement)

- "Happy to jump on a quick call at some point and run through a few ideas if helpful."
- "Could probably do a quick audit and see where the biggest bottlenecks are."
- "Happy to take a quick look at how things are currently running and see what could probably be cleaned up a bit."
- "We've helped a few similar businesses simplify this sort of stuff recently. Happy to run through what we've seen working."

---

## Final rule

The conversation should always feel **low pressure, calm, natural, and experience-based**.

The call should feel like **a practical conversation about operations** — not a sales meeting.
